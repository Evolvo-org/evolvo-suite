# Evolvo V3 Governor and Aftercare

## Purpose

This document defines the two continuous oversight loops in V3:

- the Governor, which supervises the entire system
- the Aftercare system, which listens to launched products and routes improvement back into delivery

These systems ensure V3 does not stop at project execution and does not become blind to what happens after launch.

## Governor

### Role
The Governor is not a normal worker.

It is the supervisory intelligence above the rest of the system.

It should:
- observe the full portfolio
- critique ongoing work
- identify weak opportunities
- detect drift and wasted effort
- highlight risk
- recommend what deserves attention
- provide continuous standup-like reporting

### Governor Input Areas
The Governor should consume signals from:
- portfolio state
- research output
- scoring trends
- approval pipeline
- execution progress
- deployment health
- commercial readiness
- feedback trends
- project performance data

### Governor Output Areas
The Governor should produce:
- warnings
- recommendations
- portfolio summaries
- weak-work detection
- stalled-project detection
- opportunity-quality feedback
- invest/watch/kill signals

### Governor Reporting Categories

#### Portfolio Health
- active ideas
- approved projects
- live products
- archived work
- unhealthy assets

#### Research Health
- evidence quality
- noise level
- promising clusters
- low-value categories

#### Execution Health
- progress
- blockers
- stall risk
- quality concerns

#### Commercial Health
- pricing readiness
- billing setup readiness
- launch blockers

#### Product Health
- recurring user issues
- retention risks
- trust/reliability concerns
- strong positive signals

### Governor Behaviour Principle
The Governor must not merely summarise events.  
It must challenge, prioritise, and critique.

## Aftercare

### Role
Aftercare is the post-launch listening and response system.

Its purpose is to:
- ingest user and product signals
- identify meaningful issues and requests
- reduce noise
- send validated work back into the project lifecycle

### Input Sources
Aftercare should be designed to ingest from:
- in-app feedback
- support tickets
- direct user messages
- surveys
- reviews
- social commentary
- analytics anomalies
- churn and downgrade reasons

### Feedback Signal Categories
Signals should be classified into:
- bugs
- UX friction
- feature requests
- monetisation issues
- onboarding issues
- retention risks
- support pain
- trust/reliability issues

### Aftercare Processing
The system should:
1. ingest raw signals
2. normalise them
3. cluster similar signals
4. detect recurrence
5. estimate severity
6. prioritise actionable items
7. route validated work into project execution

### Aftercare Outputs
- prioritised issue candidates
- validated feature opportunities
- retention-risk alerts
- quality warnings
- backlog-ready work items

## Governor and Aftercare Relationship

Aftercare feeds real-world product signals upward to the Governor.

The Governor then uses those signals to:
- detect weak launches
- identify products needing attention
- critique missed follow-through
- highlight valuable improvement work

## Success Criteria

Governor and Aftercare are working when:
- important signals surface quickly
- noise is reduced
- recurring problems are detected reliably
- product improvements flow back into active work
- Patrick gets honest, useful oversight of both execution and outcomes
